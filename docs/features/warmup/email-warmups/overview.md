- **Email Content Guidelines**: Use conversational, personal emails
- **Timing Optimization**: Send emails during business hours (9 AM - 5 PM)
- **List Quality**: Use verified, high-quality contact lists

<details>
<summary><strong>Level 2: Professional Practices</strong></summary>

### **Level 2: Professional Practices**

## Content Guidelines

- Use conversational, personal emails
- Avoid obvious sales language initially
- Include relevant, valuable content
- Keep messages short and engaging
- Use proper grammar and spelling

## Timing Optimization

- Send emails during business hours (9 AM - 5 PM)
- Avoid weekends for business outreach
- Consider your target audience's time zone
- Space emails appropriately throughout the day
- Test different send times to find optimal windows

## List Quality

- Use verified, high-quality contact lists
- Remove bounced or invalid email addresses
- Segment lists by engagement level
- Avoid purchasing low-quality email lists
- Regularly clean and update your contacts

## Common Warm-up Mistakes to Avoid

### **Level 1: Critical Mistakes**

**Major errors that destroy deliverability:**

- **Sending Too Fast**: Starting with high volumes (100+ emails) immediately
- **Poor Content Quality**: Sales-focused content during warm-up damages reputation
- **Inconsistent Patterns**: Irregular sending patterns flag accounts as automated

<details>
<summary><strong>Level 2: Advanced Mistakes</strong></summary>

### **Level 2: Advanced Mistakes**

## Timing Errors

- **Ignoring Time Zones**: Not considering prospect time zones
- **Weekend Sending**: Sending business emails on weekends
- **Holiday Overlap**: Not accounting for holidays and business closures
- **Frequency Mistakes**: Sending too frequently or with irregular patterns

## Content Mistakes

- **Generic Templates**: Using one-size-fits-all templates
- **Poor Personalization**: Insufficient or incorrect personalization
- **Link-Heavy Content**: Including too many links in warm-up emails
- **Attachment Issues**: Sending attachments before reputation is established

## Technical Mistakes

- **DNS Issues**: Improper SPF, DKIM, or DMARC configuration
- **Domain Problems**: Using suspicious or low-quality domains
- **IP Reputation**: Not monitoring IP reputation across providers
- **List Quality**: Using purchased or outdated contact lists

</details>
